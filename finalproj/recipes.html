Rolls

The following two recipes can be made into cinnamon, orange or plain dinner rolls.

REFRIGERATOR ROLLS

Must start this recipe in the morning or night before as the dough rises in the refrigerator.

Mix together:

1 cup shortening

2 t. salt

¾ cup sugar

Add:

1 ½ cup warm water

2 envelopes of yeast = 4 ½ teaspoons

4 eggs well beaten

Add alternately:

7 ½ cups flour

1 cup cold water

Dough will be sticky. Place in large greased bowl, cover and refrigerate overnight. Or mix in the morning and refrigerate all day. Divide dough in half when you get ready t roll them out. Use lots of flour. Roll out for dinner rolls in a round circle, cut 16 out of each circle. For cinnamon or orange, roll in a large rectangle, spread with desired filling. Let rise until almost double, bake 375 degrees for 10-12 minutes.

Carmelitas

Ingredients:

1 cup of flour

1 cup of Quaker oats

½ tsp. baking soda

¼ tsp. salt

¾ cup of brown sugar

Mix together with a fork.

Add:

¾ cup of melted butter

Mix again. Mixture will be crumbly.

Spread half of the mixture in a greased 9x13 pan. Press the mixture into the bottom of the pan with your hands if you need to.

Bake at 350 for 10 minutes.

While this is baking, unwrap and melt over medium heat:

32 light Kraft caramels (or if you have the loaf of caramel I double the amount)

5 Tablespoons evaporated milk

Stir continuously to avoid burning.

Remove pan for the oven and sprinkle on:

1 cup of chocolate chips

1 cup of chopped pecans

Pour on the melted caramel mixture. Sprinkle remaining crumbly mixture on top and return to oven for another 15 minutes.
